Darwinian Natural Selection

  • Heredity - children receive the properties of their parents
  • Variation - there must be a variety of traits present in the population
  • Selection - “survival of the fittest”
  • Step one - Initialization
  • Step two - Selection
  • Step three - Reproduction
Step 1: Initialize. Create a population of N elements, each with randomly generated DNA.
Step 2: Selection. Evaluate the fitness of each element of the population and build a mating pool.
Step 3: Reproduction. Repeat N times: a) Pick two parents with probability according to relative fitness. b) Crossover—create a “child” by combining the DNA of these two parents. c) Mutation—mutate the child’s DNA based on a given probability. d) Add the new child to a new population. Replace the old population with the new population and return to Step 2.
